What are Performance Dimensions and how are they useful?


The 22 Performance Dimensions (PDs) are definitions of on-the-job behaviors demonstrated by effective Nortel Networks employees and managers. They apply to all jobs at all band levels. These definitions provide measurable criteria to help you understand the behaviors that can be developed to improve performance and, potentially, your job and employee satisfaction. Each PD consists of four levels of mastery. To see the content of the PDs, see the Performance Dimensions Development Map (Click here to download the PDF version).

What is a mastery level?


A Performance Dimension has four levels of mastery, with level 1 being the most basic and 4 being the most complex. It is important to know, however, that level 1 is not considered "poor" and level 4 is not considered "good." The rating represents a specified level of expertise so that you can identify the required PD mastery level needed for any role. The levels are cumulative; people who operate at level 3, therefore, also consistently demonstrate the skills required at levels 1 and 2.

Where did the PDs come from?


The PDs were created by a global group of employees from four sources:
  • Existing competency-based models in use in Nortel Networks
  • Interviews with senior executives
  • The research of an external consultant, Hay/McBer
  • Feedback data from customer satisfaction surveys

Are PDs part of the Performance Management (Priorities) process?


By including the use of Performance Dimensions in setting expectations and identifying development activities, both the employee and the employer can focus on the key behaviors that are required for a role. Evaluation and feedback based on the PDs is more specific and easily understood, and the behaviors that are needed can be measured objectively. For help in identifying development activities for specific PDs, see the Nortel Networks' Employee Training PD Development Map (Click here to download the PDF version).

How do the PDs relate to Job Complexity Indicator (JCI)?

The Performance Dimensions are not related to JCI. They are not a job evaluation system, and they are not linked to compensation. They are the measurable performance criteria which any employee can display to develop and improve their performance.

Can I assume that higher band equates to higher PD mastery levels?


No. In fact, it is possible that someone at band 5 may need a higher level of mastery for effective performance of his/her job than someone at band 8. For example, a band 5 Engineer may need a far higher PD level of analysis than a band 8 manager. It is outputs or accountabilities of the role which are key to selecting both the right PD and the appropriate mastery level.
